Treatments

Eight ways Dr Li treats the whole person.

Every treatment plan is discussed and priced at your consultation — Dr Li tailors the combination of methods below to your condition, not the other way round.

鍼

Acupuncture

Very fine, sterilised needles are inserted into the skin at points along the body's channels to regulate the flow of energy. Needles are left in place for 20–30 minutes.

草

Chinese herbal medicine

Combinations of herbs, selected from a wide range to treat specific conditions, taken as tea, pills, tablets, capsules or oral liquid.

罐

Cupping

A jar is placed on the skin surface to relieve local congestion, improve blood circulation and help detoxify the system.

刮

Gua Sha

Oil is applied over the body, then a piece of wood is expertly moved down the skin to eliminate toxins and ease tension.

耳

Ear candling

A gentle treatment used to help remove earwax, unblock ears, and ease tinnitus and hearing difficulties.

美

Cosmetic acupuncture

Very thin, disposable needles inserted at points on the face to stimulate collagen production and reduce visible signs of ageing.

育

Acupuncture to induce labour

A safe, natural way to help support labour, recommended for mothers from 40 weeks of pregnancy onwards.

顔

Acupuncture for Bell's Palsy

Targeted acupuncture treatment for facial paralysis and related nerve symptoms.

Qigong & meditation training

Beyond the treatment room.

Alongside acupuncture and herbal medicine, Dr Li teaches Qigong — a moving-meditation practice she began training in with a Buddhist teacher in 1990, reaching Qigong and Meditation Master status by 1995. She maintains a daily meditation practice herself and shares it with clients as part of ongoing care, particularly for stress, sleep and general wellbeing.

Before you book

Common questions

How much does treatment cost?

Pricing depends on the treatment and your individual plan, and is discussed with Dr Li at your consultation before any treatment begins.

What should I expect at a first visit?

Dr Li will talk through your condition and history, then recommend a treatment or combination of treatments — acupuncture, herbal medicine, cupping or Gua Sha — suited to you.

Do you treat pregnancy-related conditions?

Yes — acupuncture to help induce labour is offered from 40 weeks of pregnancy onwards. Speak to Dr Li directly about timing and suitability.

Is Dr Li a member of a professional body?

Yes — Dr Li has been a member of the Association of Traditional Chinese Medicine and Acupuncture, UK (ATCM) since 2005.

Ready to talk through your options?

Get in touch and Dr Li will help you decide which treatment fits your condition.
